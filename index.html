Grant Thornton AWS Solutions Group

The Grant Thornton AWS Solution Group accelerates end-to-end adoption of AWS, securely maximizing enterprise-wide transformation, innovation, and business value delivery at speed and at scale.

Capabilities and Service Offerings

Grant Thornton helps clients manage data as a valuable resource. Grant Thornton uses the AWS Database Migration Service to execute homogeneous (e.g. Oracle to Oracle) and heterogeneous (e.g. MS SQL to AWS Aurora) database cloud migrations. This service includes delivery of cloud-based data management services, including data governance, data architecture, data modeling and design, data security, master data management, data integration, data ETL and ELT, data warehousing, data lakes, enterprise business intelligence, data quality and other services. Grant Thornton delivers this service using AWS database, storage, migration and transfer, content delivery, analytics, machine learning and related services.

Grant Thornton helps clients assess their cloud foundation capabilities and maturity and guide client infrastructure and operations (I&O) technical professionals (who are responsible for enterprise cloud migration to IaaS and PaaS) through requirements that enterprises should address in order to support the demands of production workloads on public cloud providers. Grant Thornton help clients understand business and I&O team responsibilities and assess and plan necessary improvements in 12 foundational areas: (1) master and sub-account hierarchies and billing (2) user identity and role-based access control (3) core IP transit and name resolution (4) object, block and file storage (5) application-specific networking (6) compute (7) PaaS services (8) IT operations management (9) continuity, availability, and disaster recovery (10) governance (11) security (12) automation

Grant Thornton helps clients establish or mature a cloud center of excellence (CCoE) that is a service and practice center with deep and wide knowledge of cloud development, operations, security, platforms, design, delivery and run best practices. Grant Thornton helps clients establish CCoEs that accelerate businesses innovation through provision of automation, templates, practices, solutions and services that support cloud adoption and usage. With the goal of improving outcomes and managing risks, Grant Thornton helps clients establish CCoEs that provide centralized governance, set cloud policy, guide provider selection, assist with solution architecture and workload placement, and raise the level of cloud knowledge and capture and disseminate best practices. CCoEs established through this service, work in a consultative capacity -- with central IT, business-unit IT, and cloud service consumers -- to help drive cloud-enabled IT transformation.

Grant Thornton helps clients understand how cloud adoption transforms the way they work, identify stakeholder barriers and gaps to cloud adoption, recognize cross-organization cloud adoption dependencies, and develop a customize a execution action plan for cloud adoption. Grant Thornton delivers this service to help clients identify and classify cloud adoption challenges from six perspectives (business, people, governance, platform, security, operations) and define and prioritize actions that need to be taken to overcome these cloud adoption challenges. The service based on AWS Cloud Adoption Framework (CAF).

Grant Thornton helps clients understand fully-loaded cloud costs. Grant Thornton delivers accurate and timely cloud spend/usage analytics that facilitate shared accountability and understanding of cloud resource usage and spend. This service includes (a) creating and applying tags and establishing account hierarchies to support cost allocation and show/chargeback (b) helping business, engineering and finance forecast cloud spend and stay within budget (c) using trend and variance analysis to identify spend anomalies.

Grant Thornton helps clients conduct cloud cost and spend benchmarking to establish key performance indicators (KPIs) and establish performance goals. Benchmarking conducted through this service offering includes internal (client) team benchmarking, which is used to identify best practices, as well as industry peer-level benchmarking, which is used to determine how client organization is performing (with regard to cloud cost optimization) relative to external organizations.

Grant Thornton helps clients optimize cloud use and related spend by identifying and documenting optimization objectives, target results, and the business case for cloud cost optimization. This service can include establishing centralized team to perform capacity reservation decision making to take advantage of saving available through reserved instances, savings plans, committed use discounting, and spot (spare capacity) resources. Through this service, Grant Thornton helps clients plan resource optimizations that involve rightsizing and eliminating idle and underused resources and workloads. Grant Thornton also provides architectural optimization advice for storage and data lifecycle management, instance size and family, managed service adoption, and containers and serverless strategies. Implementation of optimization automations, including provisioning, alarming, budgeting, cost tracking and estimation automations are part of this service offering.

Grant Thornton helps clients optimize resource usage and manage cloud spend on an on-going basis. This service typically involves establishing a centralized FinOps team that collaborates with the client's cloud COE. Through this service, Grant Thornton establishes a cadence for on-going collaboration with business, engineering, and finance stakeholders, continually adjusts and executes optimization plans and automates resource turn-off/on and cloud use/spend analysis and reporting. Grant Thornton also continuously monitors spend and usage trends and forecasts and adjusts optimization strategies. On an on-going basis, Grant Thornton also monitors and reports on cloud unit economics to help the client demonstrate stewardship and continuous improvement.

Grant Thornton helps clients structure their cloud governance processes so they can maintain control over cloud resource usage, visibility, and policy enforcement as their cloud accounts and cloud usage grows. Grant Thornton helps clients set up and govern and secure multi-account AWS environments by establishing a landing zone that is based on best-practices blueprints, and enables governance using guardrails. Through this service, Grant Thornton automates the creation of a landing zone with best-practices blueprints that configure AWS Organizations for a multi-account structure, provide identity management using AWS SSO Directory, provide federated access using AWS Single Sign-On (AWS SSO), create a central log archive using AWS CloudTrail and AWS Config, enable security audits across accounts using AWS SSO, implement network configurations using Amazon VPC, and define workflows for provisioning accounts using AWS Service Catalog. As part of this service, Grant Thornton also helps clients establish “guardrails” for ongoing governance of their AWS environment. Guardrails provide governance controls by preventing deployment of resources that don’t conform to selected policies or detecting non-conformance of provisioned resources. Guardrails are implemented using services, such as AWS CloudFormation to establish a baseline, AWS Organizations service control policies (SCPs) to prevent configuration changes, and AWS Config rules to continuously detect non-conformance. To deliver this service, Grant Thornton uses (1) AWS Control Tower to implement pre-defined blueprints and guardrails to help customers implement a landing zone for new AWS accounts, or (2) AWS Control Tower to provide a fully configurable, customer-managed landing zone implementation.

Grant Thornton helps clients plan, prioritize and prepare for application rationalization and cloud migrations. Grant Thornton works with clients to (a) establish application rationalization and cloud migration decision-making processes, roles and responsibilities, and working groups from across the organization; (b) leverage Capital Planning and Investment Control (CPIC) processes, tools and information to define the scope of application rationalization efforts; (c) inventory applications; (d) align applications with TBM service layer and analyze costs, business value, financial, and technical information; (e) use business impact analysis (BIA) to rate application business impacts and document technical disposition, dependencies, and duplicates; (f) estimate and forecast total cost of application on-premise operations; (f) define application rationalization strategies (review, reward, refresh, remove); and (g) prioritize application migrations based on application cost estimates, BIA, and technical disposition findings. Note is service performed when a portfolio of application migrations must be planned and prioritized.

Where clients have identified an on-premise application as a candidate for migration to the cloud, Grant Thornton helps clients develop a business case and implementation plan for the proposed migration. As part of this service, Grant Thornton helps client (a) conduct analysis of cloud migration alternatives, to include consideration of cloud providers, solutions and services; (b) leverage cloud migration assessment calculators to estimate total cost of migration (TCM) and total cost of cloud operations (TCO); (c) use cloud migration calculators to quantify business value (e.g. operational resilience, workforce productivity, business agility) enabled by migration; (d) define application migration strategy (e.g. re-purchase, re-host, re-platform, re-factor, re-architect); (e) develop reference architecture for application to be migrated; (f) develop and validate business case for application migration; and (g) define implementation plan and timeline for application migration.

Where clients have approved business case for application cloud migration, or otherwise decided to migrate an on-premise application to the cloud, Grant Thornton works with clients to execute cloud migration strategies (e.g. re-purchase, re-host, re-platform, re-factor, re-architect) and implementation plans. As part of this service, Grant Thornton works with clients to (a) establish current (pre-migration) performance baselines; (b) establish key performance indicators (KPIs) for the migrated (cloud) application (e.g. user experience (page load, response time), application performance (availability, throughput), infrastructure (resource utilization and cost); (c) develop dependency diagrams to document dependencies between the application to be migrated and other on-premise and in-cloud workloads; (d) use understanding of dependencies to prioritize migration workload services and components; (e) perform re-factoring required for migrated application to run effectively and predictably in the cloud; (f) create data migration strategy and plan and select cloud provider data migration tools and services; (g) conduct migration engineering, validate, monitor operations and optimize resource utilization and spend.

Grant Thornton helps clients architect and plan the implementation of new workloads (application, service, capability, etc.). Grant Thornton uses the AWS Well-Architected Framework to evaluate existing workload architectures. When designing new workload architectures, Grant Thornton considers five categories of well-architected best practice: (1) Operational Excellence - Ability to support development and run workloads effectively, gain insight into their operations, and to continuously improve; (2) Security - Ability to protect data, systems, and assets to take advantage of cloud technologies to improve security posture; (3) Reliability - Ability of a workload to perform its intended function correctly and consistently when it’s expected throughout its total lifecycle; (4) Performance Efficiency - Ability to use resources efficiently to meet system requirements, and to maintain that efficiency as demand changes and technologies evolve; (5) Cost Optimization - Ability to run systems that satisfy QoS standards and deliver business value at the lowest price point.

Grant Thornton uses the AWS Well-Architected Framework to evaluate existing workload architectures. As part of this service, Grant Thornton performs premortem exercises to identify potential sources of failure so that they can be mitigated and tests failure scenarios to validate impact understanding and test response procedures to make sure they are effective and can be executed, as planned.

Grant Thornton helps clients execute the Assessment and Authorization (A&A) security authorization process for cloud workloads. This service is delivered through collaboration between professionals within the Grant Thornton Public Sector Risk, Information Assurance and Cloud teams. Grant Thornton helps clients secure official management decision to authorize operation of cloud-based workloads and to secure explicit acceptance of risk to agency operations (including mission, functions, image, or reputation), agency assets, individuals, other organizations, and the Nation based on the implementation of an agreed-upon set of security and privacy controls and common controls inherited from the cloud platform. As part of this service, Grant Thornton helps clients secure (a) Interim ATO - a conditional ATO, generally in effect for six months, often during the development or prototype; (b) Initial ATO - must be done prior to the workload going live and must occur at least every three years thereafter; (c) Reauthorization - due after three years or a significant change to the system's risk level. Grant Thornton provides this service for FISMA reportable systems, including general support systems (GSS), financial reporting systems, and major applications (MA). Grant Thornton follows the six step ATO security authorization process defined in the NIST Risk Management Framework (categorize, select, implement, assess, authorize, and monitor). When delivering this service, Grant Thornton considers 18 control families (e.g. access control, audit and accountability, personnel security, media protection) within three control classes (e.g. management, operational, technical), as defined within NIST RMF and related standards, including NIST SP 800-(37, 53, 60, 137) and FIPS 199 and 200.
